Radius - environment made social

Tweet

Created by Wyatt Komarin

I'm a Designer in New York, NY who is currently freelancing.

Description

Radius is an app that shows you how you and your friends on facebook are impacted by environmental issues - be it an oil spill, a polluted river, or a climate change event. Radius transforms typically dull and impersonal environmental issues into a social experience, making them relevant to your life, and spurring you to take action in the process.

We asked a simple question: What is the biggest environmental challenge facing society today? You might say climate change, air pollution, or some other challenge. We believe the biggest challenge is something more fundamental than these.

Underlying all of these issues is the serious problem of Environmental Apathy - we've become indifferent as a society to environmental ills, and as a result there are few big actions taken on any environmental issue. We believe Radius is the way to combat this apathy, spurring interest in cleantech, and generating concern about the environment by connecting the environment to those you care about.

Radius was a top ten idea (out of 150 entries from 25 different countries) in the Facebook "Cleantech Goes Social" competition held this February.

We are looking for a developer who sees the potential impact that Radius can have, and ideally someone who has knowledge of mapping systems, particularly in the integration of numerous spatial data sets.


Potential Data Sets:


Geographic Environmental Data Sets:

EPA API: http://www.epa.gov/enviro/facts/topicsearch.html#air

Coal Plants: http://en.openei.org/wiki/Coal_Power_Plant_Database

PV Farm Locations: https://openpv.nrel.gov/

Wind Farm Locations: http://archive.awea.org/projects/

Polluted Waterways: http://projects.nytimes.com/toxic-waters/polluters/Connecticut

Toxic Waste Sites (EPA Superfund Sites): http://projects.nytimes.com/toxic-
waters/polluters/Connecticut

Drought Areas: http://droughtmonitor.unl.edu/

Goals

  • Change the way people think about "the environment" at large.
  • Build a functioning version of Radius and potentially pitch concept to environmental advocacy organizations like WWF.
